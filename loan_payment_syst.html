Bi-Weekly Payment System

According to "The Automatic Millionaire" by David Bach

The bi-weekly payment system is a powerful financial strategy that can help you pay off your mortgage faster and save a significant amount of money in interest over the life of the loan. This system is prominently featured in David Bach's book, "The Automatic Millionaire," where he explains how making bi-weekly payments can accelerate your path to financial freedom.

In a traditional monthly payment system, you make one payment each month, resulting in 12 payments per year. However, with the bi-weekly payment system, you make a payment every two weeks. Since there are 52 weeks in a year, this results in 26 bi-weekly payments, which is equivalent to 13 monthly payments. This extra payment each year can make a significant difference in the amount of interest you pay and the time it takes to pay off your loan.

The concept behind the bi-weekly payment system is simple but effective. By making more frequent payments, you reduce the principal balance of your loan faster. This, in turn, reduces the amount of interest that accrues on the loan. Over time, the savings can be substantial, and you can pay off your mortgage years ahead of schedule.

One of the key advantages of the bi-weekly payment system is that it aligns with many people's pay schedules. If you are paid bi-weekly, making bi-weekly mortgage payments can be more convenient and easier to manage. It also helps to create a disciplined approach to paying off your mortgage, as the payments are automatically deducted from your account every two weeks.

David Bach emphasizes the importance of automation in his book. By setting up automatic bi-weekly payments, you remove the temptation to spend the money elsewhere and ensure that your mortgage payments are always made on time. This "set it and forget it" approach is a cornerstone of "The Automatic Millionaire" philosophy, which advocates for automating your finances to build wealth effortlessly.

To illustrate the impact of the bi-weekly payment system, let's consider an example. Suppose you have a $200,000 mortgage with a 30-year term and an interest rate of 4%. With a traditional monthly payment system, your monthly payment would be approximately $955. By the end of the 30 years, you would have paid a total of $343,739, with $143,739 going towards interest.

Now, let's switch to the bi-weekly payment system. Instead of making one monthly payment of $955, you make a bi-weekly payment of $477.50. Over the course of a year, you make 26 bi-weekly payments, which is equivalent to 13 monthly payments. By making this extra payment each year, you reduce the principal balance faster and pay less interest over the life of the loan.

With the bi-weekly payment system, you would pay off your mortgage in approximately 25 years instead of 30. The total amount paid would be $311,576, with $111,576 going towards interest. This results in a savings of $32,163 in interest and paying off your mortgage five years earlier.

The bi-weekly payment system is not limited to mortgages. It can also be applied to other types of loans, such as car loans, student loans, and personal loans. The principle remains the same: by making more frequent payments, you reduce the principal balance faster and save on interest.

Implementing the bi-weekly payment system is relatively straightforward. Many lenders offer bi-weekly payment options, and you can set up automatic payments through your bank or mortgage servicer. If your lender does not offer a bi-weekly payment option, you can achieve the same effect by making an extra payment each year or by dividing your monthly payment by 12 and adding that amount to each monthly payment.

It's important to note that not all lenders accept bi-weekly payments, and some may charge fees for setting up a bi-weekly payment plan. Before making any changes to your payment schedule, it's essential to check with your lender and understand any potential fees or restrictions.
